Getting a perfect sear on salmon is one of those fundamental skills that elevates a home meal to a restaurant-quality experience. Here's how I do it:

1. **Dry the skin**: Use a paper towel to pat the skin completely dry. Moisture is the enemy of crispiness.
2. **Cold pan? No**: Get your pan hot first with a high-smoke point oil.
3. **Press it down**: Once the salmon hits the pan, press it gently for about 30 seconds to ensure even contact.

Mastering this will change how you view seafood forever.
